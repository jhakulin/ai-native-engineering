# Workshop: Becoming an AI-Native Engineering Organization

**Format:** Full day, face-to-face  
**Cadence:** Monthly  
**Participants:** AI engineers, architects, and selected product, quality, and security representatives

## Purpose

Create a shared view of what AI-native engineering should mean for us, where we are now, and which concrete steps would improve the current situation.

## Targets For The Day

- Align on the target direction for AI-native engineering.
- Map the biggest gaps between today and that target.
- Identify where current ways of working, tooling, ownership, and security slow us down.
- Explore how engineers, product owners, architects, QA, and AI agents should work together.
- Identify practical improvements teams can try immediately.
- Agree on a few next steps, owners, and follow-up actions.

## Suggested Flow

1. **Where Are We Going?**  
   Define the desired AI-native engineering direction.

2. **Where Are We Now?**  
   Discuss current blockers: adoption stalls, unclear ownership, tooling friction, SDD heaviness, Jira/spec/repo questions, security gaps, and lack of proven value.

3. **What Needs To Change?**  
   Identify changes in engineering roles, team workflows, product/QA collaboration, agent ownership, guardrails, proof, and review.

4. **What Can We Try Next?**  
   Choose concrete experiments for the next month.

5. **What Do We Commit To?**  
   Pick actions, owners, and a follow-up rhythm.

## Workshop Principle

Keep it practical:

> Every major discussion should lead to a decision, an experiment, or an explicitly owned open question.
